# Style Guide for High-Performance LinkedIn Posts

## Hook Patterns

High-performing posts consistently open with:

- **Contrarian statements**
  - “Most people are using AI wrong”
  - Challenges common assumptions

- **Specific outcomes**
  - “$0 to $60K in 3 weeks”
  - “4 hours → 20 minutes”

- **System-level observations**
  - Focus on how work actually gets done, not opinions

Rules:
- No generic openings
- No “AI is changing everything”
- The first line must create tension or curiosity

---

## Structure Rules

Posts follow a highly scannable format:

- 1 sentence per line (dominant pattern)
- 8–20 lines total
- Heavy use of spacing to control pacing

Common structure:

1. Hook  
2. Context (1–3 lines)  
3. Breakdown (bullets or steps)  
4. Insight or shift  
5. Takeaway  

Formatting patterns:

- Bullet points for systems and workflows  
- Short blocks for readability  
- No dense paragraphs  

---

## Content Patterns

High-performing posts are built around:

### 1. Real Workflows
- Step-by-step systems
- Clear transformation (before vs after)
- Focus on execution, not theory

### 2. Small vs Large Team Contrast
- Coordination vs speed
- Systems vs headcount
- Output vs effort

### 3. Operational Insights
- “What actually works”
- “What most teams get wrong”
- Based on experience, not speculation

### 4. System Thinking
- Emphasis on processes, not tools
- AI as part of a workflow, not a standalone feature

---

## Credibility Techniques

Posts establish credibility through:

- **Specific numbers**
  - time saved
  - revenue generated
  - team size

- **Concrete examples**
  - real workflows
  - edge cases
  - bugs or failures

- **Tradeoffs**
  - what didn’t work
  - limitations of the system

Important:
Credibility comes from specificity, not authority claims.

---

## Tone & Voice

The dominant tone is:

- Direct and assertive  
- Experience-based  
- Minimal filler  

Key characteristics:

- Sounds like an operator, not a marketer  
- Avoids hype language  
- Uses simple, clear phrasing  

Examples of tone:

- “Here’s what changed”
- “This wasn’t a demo”
- “It’s not perfect, but…”

---

## Anti-Patterns

Avoid:

- Generic statements
  - “AI is powerful”
  - “The future is exciting”

- Motivational content
- Thought leadership without substance
- Over-explaining concepts

Critical rule:

If the post cannot be applied in practice, it is low quality.

---

## Ending Patterns

High-performing endings:

- **Reframes**
  - “You don’t need more people. You need better systems.”

- **Operational conclusions**
  - Shift from tools → systems
  - Shift from effort → leverage

- **Concise takeaways**
  - No long conclusions
  - No forced CTA

Important:

Most top posts do NOT rely on:
- “Comment below”
- “What do you think?”

They end with clarity, not engagement bait.
